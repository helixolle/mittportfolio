2Gether2.0

2023, 10 weeks

Project Brief

2Gether2.0 is a platform designed to empower digital creators by providing a fair and transparent environment for them to share their work and receive proper recognition and compensation.

The Problem

Many online platforms fail to provide digital creators with fair compensation for their work. Creators often struggle to gain recognition and build a sustainable income.

The Goal

The goal of this project was to design a user-friendly and intuitive mobile app for 2Gether2.0 that empowers digital creators and fosters a supportive community.

Team

Project done individually.
I was responsible for all aspects of the design process.

Tools

Figma Pen&Paper

The Process

The main focus for this project was to understand certain interface patterns and integrate them into a fictional service, therefore was no extensive user research or testing with real users to be done.

User Research

Persona

A persona named Johanna Norberg was created to represent the target audience - a UX design student who creates and shares digital content.

Context Scenarios

Three scenarios were developed to explore how Johanna would use the 2Gether2.0 app: finding a 3D modeling course, uploading a painting, and starting a new photo project.

Design

Sketches

I started out by sketching the main page for each context scenario. The sketches shown below are iterations of many discarded sketches.

Paper prototype

I created an 'interactive' paper prototype out of a spiral bound sketch book that had the approximate size of a smartphone. I tried to create this prototype to be as "reusable" as possible, meaning that my different scenarios crossed each other and therefore could several pages be resuable in the prototype. Making it interactive enough to get a better sense of how the service would work.

Here are three videos going through each scenario using the paper prototype (in swedish).

Wireframes

Based on the paper prototype I created wireframes where some smaller adjustments have been done in comparison with the paper prototype. These were mainly around placements of buttons and captions.

Wireframes made in Figma.

Mockup

In this phase I created four mockups for key pages of 2Gether2.0: the homepage, search results page, product page, and profile page. The aim was to establish a modern and consistent design that aligns with the target audience's needs.

Graphical mockups for key pages made in Figma.

Interactive prototype

Building from the wireframes and mockups, an interactive prototype was created. This prototype allows users to experience core functionalities like searching, filtering, and navigating the app. By enacting the context scenarios within the prototype, I could validate the user flow and ensure a smooth and intuitive experience.

Screenshots of the interactive prototype made in Figma.
