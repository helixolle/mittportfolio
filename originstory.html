OriginStory

2023, 10 weeks

Project Brief

This project aimed to develop OriginStory, a browser extension designed to empower news consumers with a deeper understanding of news narratives.

The Problem

The modern news landscape is complex and often fragmented. Consumers struggle to track the evolution of news stories, identify bias, and discern the credibility of information.

The Goal

The goal was to create a user-friendly tool that allows news consumers to explore the relationships between different news sources covering the same story.

Team

This project was done individually.

Tools

Figma Illustrator

The Process

The main focus for this project was to understand certain interface patterns and integrate them into a fictional service, therefore was no extensive user research or testing with real users to be done.

User Research

Brainstorming

With a description of the service provided by our teacher I began to brainstorm about what users could benefit from this service. After this session I discussed the result with my teacher and two of my classmates. During this session I also brainstormed around different needs and certain functions the service could have. It also resulted with three simple persona descriptions.

Target Group

The primary target audience has been news consumers, this is a diverse audience that consists of a lot of other different people in different fields. My focus has been to provide a tool that is available for ‘regular’ people that consume news online, but the service is certainly relevant to journalists, researchers, fact checkers, as well.

By catering to regular people who consume news online, OriginStory ensures accessibility for individuals with varying levels of familiarity with news analysis tools. Whether you are a casual news reader or a professional journalist, OriginStory offers a user-friendly experience that promotes critical analysis.

In summary, while the primary target remains news consumers, OriginStory functions as a tool for a broader audience, including journalists, researchers, and anyone interested in gaining a deeper understanding of how news stories evolve over time.

Brainstorming around users along with their needs and features that they would want.

Design

Sketches

I started out with sketching a low fidelity design of the service and from these sketches I could recognize certain interface patterns. After these patterns emerged I translated my sketches into wireframes in Figma so I could get a better understanding of the layout and further make some improvements in the design.

While sketching the main page I also sketched variations on the 'filter box', as shown below. These sketches eventually also turned into a wireframes, also shown below.

A dashboard interface pattern started to emerge.
Different variations on some sort of 'filter section'.

Wireframes

While creating the wireframes I could recognize two interface patterns in my design that I tried to expand on, the two patterns are:

- Dashboard View

This pattern provides a central or 'main' location for users to access key information and features. In OriginStory, this view displays the map of interconnected news sources.

- Canvas Plus Palette

This pattern structures the interface with a central workspace (the canvas) and surrounding tools (the palette) used to manipulate the content in the canvas. In OriginStory, the map view acts as the canvas, and the filter panel acts as the palette for refining the view.

A dashboard and canvas plus palette inspired interface pattern started to emerge.

Interactive prototype

The “main” page of OriginStory let users see a condensed but comprehensive view of key information in the form of a map-view. This view allows users to navigate interconnected nodes of news stories in an efficient and visual way.

Main page / Dashboard

Opening a node provides the user with information regarding that specific node and also lets the user comment the node or upvote/downvote other users comments.

Clicking the filter button provides the user with different filters they can apply to further narrow down their search or find certain aspects or angles in their research of news stories.

While creating the interactive prototype I also integrated two other interface patterns, those were:
- Animated Transitions
This pattern utilizes subtle animations to enhance user experience and provide feedback during interactions. Some transitions in OriginStory i've added animations to are hover states, selection states, and zooming within the map view.

- Spinners and Loading Indicators
This pattern provides visual cues to users when the system is processing information or loading content. In OriginStory have I included a spinner animation when users apply filters to the map view.

Aestethics

OriginStory's aesthetic design blends the image of traditional newspapers with the off-white background, reminiscent of aged paper, and the off-black text to ensure a color palette for a minimalistic and readable interface.

These design choices aims to make OriginStory’s users to easily be able to grasp the service, and create a sense of professionalism and efficiency. I also made these choices to make the user feel that the service is almost a part of the browser's core functionality.

The logo consists of the bold letters “OS” that have different parts interconnected, this is meant to symbolize the connectivity of the different news sources. The intentional separation within the different parts of the logo is meant to reflect that not all sources are connected or they might be so far off from its original source that it is almost separated from the news story completely, by for instance a total shift in terminology or tone.
